# Design System: Toy Kingdom — The Amazing Toy Store
**Project ID:** [Pending Creation]

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
The atmosphere is undeniably vibrant, magical, and bouncy—completely defying gravity. The aesthetic philosophy revolves around an "Antigravity" environment where elements feel physically suspended in the air. It is airy, bright, and strictly avoids dark or sci-fi themes. Instead, it leans into a joyful, toy-like density where emojis flow upwards like bubbles, and interaction creates immediate, cheerful physical feedback like springy bounces.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
* **Toy-Box Red (#E31E24):** Used for primary actions, prominent call-to-action buttons ("ADD TO CART"), SALE badges, and active navigation indicators. It drives energy and attention.
* **Sunshine Yellow (#FFD700):** Used for secondary accents, "NEW" product badges, secondary buttons ("VIEW SALE"), and energetic highlights like underlines or warning texts.
* **Deep Navy (#1A237E):** Used as the primary anchor color for all body text, headings, and detailed descriptions to maintain high legibility against bright backgrounds.
* **Playful Blue (#1565C0):** Functional accent color used occasionally for category indicators or specific logo lettering.
* **Grassy Green (#388E3C):** Functional accent color used for positive reinforcement, such as the "Free Shipping" success tier.
* **Royal Gold (#C8A96E):** Highly specific detail color reserved exclusively for the definitive Toy Kingdom Crown logo.
* **Canvas White (#FFFFFF):** The absolute foundational background for surface elements, cards, and headers to keep the interface feeling bright, clean, and spacious.

## 3. Typography Rules
* **Fredoka One:** Exclusively utilized for all main headings (H1-H6), the logo, navigation links, and primary buttons. Its heavily rounded, chunky weight perfectly complements the bouncy, toy-like aesthetic.
* **Nunito:** Used for all body text, product descriptions, pricing numerals, and fine print. It provides excellent readability while maintaining rounded terminals that echo the friendly vibe of Fredoka One.

## 4. Component Stylings
* **Buttons:** Generously pill-shaped (fully rounded ends). Standard buttons are filled with Toy-Box Red and feature a slight, buoyant box-shadow. On hover, they exhibit a distinct upward physical bounce (spring physics).
* **Cards/Containers:** Feature extremely soft, heavily rounded corners (16px to 24px) for a quintessential "toy" shape. They rest on a Canvas White background with whisper-soft, diffused, slightly blue-tinted shadows. On hover, the cards lift dramatically higher, and the shadows expand and deepen, simulating a release of gravity.
* **Inputs/Forms:** Generously padded with soft, rounded corners (12px to 16px). They sit on a very faint gray backdrop and gain a vibrant colored border stroke (Playful Blue) when focused, dropping the grey background for pure white.
* **Badges:** Tilted aggressively off-axis (-5 degrees) featuring stark contrast combinations (Sunshine Yellow background with Toy-Box Red text for "NEW", Toy-Box Red background with Canvas White text for "SALE").

## 5. Layout Principles
The layout principles rely heavily on rhythmic grids (up to 4-columns on desktop) that collapse smoothly on mobile. Whitespace is generous to allow the floating "Antigravity Particle System" (stars and emojis drifting upwards in the background) to remain visible. Components follow a staggered spatial relationship where child elements fade in and bob uniquely on distinct loops, creating a deliberately misaligned, organic vertical motion across horizontal rows.
